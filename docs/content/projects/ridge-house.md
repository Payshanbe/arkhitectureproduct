## Short Summary

Ridge House is a hillside residence shaped by terrain, long rooflines, and protected terraces. The architecture follows the land rather than dominating it, framing valley views through a restrained material and spatial language.

## Long Editorial Description

Ridge House begins with the line of the hill. The residence is arranged as a long, low structure that follows the terrain, allowing the building to sit quietly within the landscape. Instead of using height or spectacle, the project relies on proportion, shadow, and framed views.

The plan stretches along the ridge, with living spaces opening toward the valley and private rooms held closer to the slope. Terraces are protected by deep roof overhangs, creating exterior rooms that remain usable throughout the day. The material palette is direct and durable: concrete, charred timber, stone, and bronze.

Inside, the house is calm and grounded. Long sightlines connect rooms without making them feel exposed. Heavy materials are balanced by warm timber and soft textiles. The project is designed for a slow relationship with landscape, where weather, distance, and changing light become part of daily life.

## Architectural Concept

A low hillside house that follows topography and uses long horizontal space to frame landscape without overpowering it.

## Lighting Concept

Deep overhangs control solar exposure and create shaded exterior rooms. Interior light is directional but soft, with valley views framed through long horizontal openings.
