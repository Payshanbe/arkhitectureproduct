# ART DIRECTION

Version 1.0

---

# Philosophy

Architecture is experienced before it is understood.

Every image on the website should communicate atmosphere before information.

The goal is not to show beautiful buildings.

The goal is to communicate restraint, proportion, material honesty, and silence.

Photography is treated as architecture itself.

---

# Emotional Direction

Every image should feel:

• Calm
• Timeless
• Quiet
• Human
• Refined
• Honest
• Architectural

Never:

• Loud
• Flashy
• Trendy
• Futuristic
• Over-processed
• Artificial
• Commercial

The website should feel like opening a premium architecture monograph.

---

# Visual Language

The visual language is based on:

• Silence
• Space
• Light
• Material
• Geometry
• Texture

Images should breathe.

Negative space is encouraged.

Objects should never compete with architecture.

---

Stone

Sand

---

# Lighting

Lighting is one of the most important design elements.

Preferred:

Soft daylight

Overcast sky

Morning light

Late afternoon

Indirect sunlight

Diffuse shadows

Avoid:

HDR

Strong contrast

Orange sunsets

Blue hour

Colored lighting

Neon

Artificial dramatic lighting

---

# Photography Style

Architecture first.

People are secondary.

Photography should never feel staged.

Preferred:

Wide architectural compositions

Long perspectives

Strong geometry

Natural framing

Quiet moments

Natural imperfections

Avoid:

Smiling people

Lifestyle photography

Fashion poses

Busy environments

Crowds

Cars

Advertisements

---

# Camera Language

Preferred lenses:

35mm

40mm

50mm

Perspective should remain natural.

Vertical lines should remain straight.

No extreme wide-angle distortion.

---

# Composition

Large areas of negative space.

Simple compositions.

One architectural idea per image.

No visual noise.

Every image should have a clear focal point.

---

# Interior Photography

Focus on:

Light

Texture

Material transitions

Joinery

Details

Furniture as architecture

Avoid:

Decor-heavy styling

Too many accessories

Visual clutter

---

# Exterior Photography

Prefer:

Strong geometry

Courtyards

Patios

Landscape integration

Natural vegetation

Weathered materials

Quiet surroundings

Avoid:

Busy streets

Traffic

Crowded cities

Advertising signs

Power lines

---

# Image Processing

Very subtle editing.

Natural white balance.

Soft contrast.

Muted saturation.

No HDR.

No excessive sharpening.

No cinematic teal-orange grading.

Images should feel timeless.

---

# Motion

Images should move slowly.

Allowed:

Fade

Soft reveal

Very subtle scale (100 → 101%)

Cross dissolve

Slow mask reveal

Not allowed:

Fast zoom

Aggressive parallax

3D spins

Bounce

Elastic

Overshoot

Flash transitions

---

# AI Generated Images

Allowed only if they satisfy all art direction rules.

They must look indistinguishable from professional architectural photography.

No obvious AI artifacts.

No impossible geometry.

No surreal environments.

---

# Portfolio Consistency

Every project should feel like it belongs to the same studio.

Different projects.

One visual language.

The portfolio should read like a single architectural publication.

---

# Image Selection Checklist

Before adding any image ask:

✓ Does this image feel calm?

✓ Does it emphasize architecture?

✓ Are materials readable?

✓ Is lighting natural?

✓ Is composition balanced?

✓ Would this image fit inside an architecture book?

✓ Does it match the previous image?

If any answer is "No"

The image should not be used.

---

# Final Principle

Less beauty.

More atmosphere.

Less decoration.

More architecture.

Less marketing.

More emotion.

Silence is a design element.
