# Signature Experience Storyboard

## Purpose

This document defines the cinematic narrative for the Version 1.1 Signature Experience.

Location:

```txt
Studio page -> Process section
```

The experience explains how architecture emerges from an idea. It should feel like watching a space come into being through thought, constraint, order, material, light, and atmosphere.

This is not a technical implementation document. It is the story and pacing guide for the future scroll experience.

---

## Core Narrative

Architecture begins as uncertainty.

At first there are only traces: questions, measurements, site conditions, habits, light, needs, silence. Through the scroll, those fragments slowly gain order. They become context. Context becomes structure. Structure receives material. Material catches light. Light settles into atmosphere.

The visitor should feel that the studio does not impose form immediately. It listens first, then clarifies.

---

## Overall Emotional Curve

## Beginning

Feeling:

```txt
quiet inquiry
```

The section begins sparse and almost unfinished. There is no building yet. The visitor sees marks, traces, and open space. The emotional tone is patient and observant.

Purpose:

- establish slowness;
- signal that design begins before form;
- invite the visitor into a thinking process.

## Middle

Feeling:

```txt
emerging order
```

The visual field becomes more legible. Lines align. Boundaries appear. A spatial framework forms. The visitor should feel that decisions are being made with care, not drama.

Purpose:

- show the transformation from research to architecture;
- make process feel precise;
- create visual momentum without spectacle.

## Climax

Feeling:

```txt
light gives meaning
```

The climax is not Structure. It is Light. Structure and material become architecture only when light activates them. This should be the most emotional shift in the sequence.

Purpose:

- make the section feel cinematic;
- reveal depth and atmosphere;
- move from technical process to human experience.

## Ending

Feeling:

```txt
calm resolution
```

The final state should feel settled. Nothing should flash or announce completion. The space simply arrives. The visitor should feel that the architecture has become inevitable.

Purpose:

- leave the viewer with stillness;
- connect process to lived atmosphere;
- end with premium restraint.

---

## Scene 01 - Research

## User Sees

An open field with very few marks.

Loose survey lines, small notations, soft points, and fragmentary measurements appear across the composition. The visual should feel like the earliest page of a sketchbook or site study.

Nothing feels resolved yet.

## User Feels

Curiosity.

The user should feel that the studio is observing before deciding. The mood is quiet, intelligent, and careful.

## Visual Change

- Empty space holds the frame.
- Thin marks appear slowly.
- A few points or traces suggest movement through a site.
- No massing, no walls, no finished geometry yet.

The visual language should be incomplete by design.

## Transition

Research begins from near-stillness.

The first marks should fade in slowly, almost like the eye adjusting to a drawing. Avoid dramatic line drawing. The transition should feel observational.

## Text

Title:

```txt
Research
```

Copy:

```txt
We begin by listening to the brief, the site, and the quiet rituals that will shape the work.
```

## Pacing

Slow introduction.

This scene should give the visitor time to understand that the section is not a normal content block. It is the opening of a process.

## Scroll Duration

Recommended desktop scroll duration:

```txt
85vh
```

Reason:

The first state needs enough time to establish silence and attention, but should not feel empty for too long.

---

## Scene 02 - Context

## User Sees

The loose marks begin to relate to place.

Boundaries appear. Edges form. A site frame, orientation marks, neighboring conditions, or directional lines give the drawing a sense of location.

The visual should now feel less like notes and more like a site reading.

## User Feels

Grounded.

The user should feel that architecture is not abstract. It responds to a place, a climate, a rhythm, and existing constraints.

## Visual Change

- A site boundary fades in.
- Context lines organize around the research marks.
- The composition gains direction.
- Negative space remains important.

The scene should clarify without becoming dense.

## Transition

Research marks remain partially visible while context lines settle around them.

The transition should communicate:

```txt
the first observations are not replaced;
they become the foundation for understanding place.
```

## Text

Title:

```txt
Context
```

Copy:

```txt
Existing conditions, orientation, climate, and daily movement define the first limits of the project.
```

## Pacing

Measured and clarifying.

The user should feel the story becoming more legible. This scene can move slightly faster than Research because the foundation has already been established.

## Scroll Duration

Recommended desktop scroll duration:

```txt
80vh
```

Reason:

Context should feel deliberate, but the experience should begin gaining momentum.

---

## Scene 03 - Structure

## User Sees

The drawing becomes architectural.

A grid, frame, wall logic, or spatial skeleton appears. The composition begins to hold proportion. It should feel like the project is finding order.

This is the first moment where the visitor can sense a future space.

## User Feels

Clarity.

The user should feel a quiet satisfaction: the fragments now have discipline. The project is no longer a collection of ideas; it has structure.

## Visual Change

- Primary structural lines appear.
- The grid becomes more intentional.
- Volumes or planes begin to define space.
- Earlier research and context layers become quieter but remain present.

The visual should not become heavy. Structure is order, not force.

## Transition

Context lines align into structural logic.

The transition can be slightly more decisive than previous scenes. This is the moment where the drawing gains confidence.

## Text

Title:

```txt
Structure
```

Copy:

```txt
Proportion, circulation, and order turn early observations into a spatial framework.
```

## Pacing

Steady and confident.

This scene is the hinge between analysis and design. It should be clear, but not abrupt.

## Scroll Duration

Recommended desktop scroll duration:

```txt
90vh
```

Reason:

Structure is a major conceptual shift. It deserves slightly more time than Context.

---

## Scene 04 - Material

## User Sees

The structural drawing gains weight.

Planes receive subtle tone. Surfaces become tactile. A quiet material field appears: stone, timber, plaster, textile, or a non-literal texture. The visual should move from line to surface.

The space begins to feel touchable.

## User Feels

Warmth.

The user should feel that the project is becoming human. Structure has logic; material gives it presence.

## Visual Change

- Selected planes fill with muted tone.
- Texture appears sparingly.
- Lines soften slightly as surfaces gain importance.
- The drawing becomes less abstract.

Material should never feel decorative. It should feel selected, restrained, and precise.

## Transition

Structure does not disappear. It receives surface.

The transition should feel like a quiet deepening rather than a new scene being pasted over the old one.

## Text

Title:

```txt
Material
```

Copy:

```txt
Surfaces are chosen for touch, ageing, restraint, and their relationship to light.
```

## Pacing

Slower and more sensory.

This scene should create a brief pause in the sequence. The visitor should feel the project becoming physical.

## Scroll Duration

Recommended desktop scroll duration:

```txt
95vh
```

Reason:

Material is where the experience becomes tactile. It benefits from a slower reveal.

---

## Scene 05 - Light

## User Sees

Light enters the composition.

A soft wash, shadow direction, or warm gradient moves across the structured material field. The drawing gains depth. Planes that previously felt flat now become spatial.

This is the emotional climax of the sequence.

## User Feels

Awakening.

The user should feel the moment architecture becomes atmosphere. The project is no longer only arranged; it is alive.

## Visual Change

- A light direction becomes visible.
- Material planes respond to light.
- Contrast increases very slightly.
- The composition gains depth and calm drama.

This should be the most cinematic transition, but still restrained.

## Transition

Light should arrive gradually, not burst.

The transition can be the most noticeable in the sequence, but it must remain premium. Think of sunlight slowly entering a quiet room.

## Text

Title:

```txt
Light
```

Copy:

```txt
Light is composed as part of the architecture, shaping rhythm, depth, and stillness.
```

## Pacing

Most cinematic.

The scroll should feel slightly suspended here. This is the peak of the emotional curve.

## Scroll Duration

Recommended desktop scroll duration:

```txt
110vh
```

Reason:

Light is the climax. It needs the longest beat so the visitor can feel the transformation.

---

## Scene 06 - Atmosphere

## User Sees

The composition resolves.

The drawing no longer feels like a process diagram. It becomes a calm spatial image. Earlier layers are still present but quiet: research, context, structure, material, and light now feel unified.

Nothing more is added. The final change is a settling.

## User Feels

Calm.

The user should feel arrival, not completion in a mechanical sense. The space has emerged naturally.

## Visual Change

- All previous layers become balanced.
- Visual contrast softens.
- The final atmosphere becomes more spacious and still.
- Motion nearly disappears.

The ending should feel like an exhale.

## Transition

Light settles into the final atmosphere.

The transition should be quieter than the Light scene. The climax has passed; this is resolution.

## Text

Title:

```txt
Atmosphere
```

Copy:

```txt
The final space is measured by how calmly it supports life, movement, and memory.
```

## Pacing

Slow resolution.

The visitor should not be pushed out immediately. Give enough scroll time for the final state to feel complete.

## Scroll Duration

Recommended desktop scroll duration:

```txt
100vh
```

Reason:

The ending should linger, but not trap the visitor after the story has resolved.

---

## Full Scroll Rhythm

Recommended desktop scroll sequence:

```txt
Research      85vh
Context       80vh
Structure     90vh
Material      95vh
Light        110vh
Atmosphere   100vh
```

Total:

```txt
560vh
```

This length should feel like a complete chapter, not a short effect. If testing shows the section feels too long, reduce each scene by 10-15vh while preserving Light as the longest beat.

---

## Text System

The text should be calm and declarative.

Avoid:

- marketing claims;
- technical jargon;
- long explanations;
- poetic excess;
- slogans.

Use:

- one title per scene;
- one short sentence per scene;
- stable text placement;
- subtle crossfade between states;
- accessible HTML for every step.

Final text set:

```txt
Research
We begin by listening to the brief, the site, and the quiet rituals that will shape the work.

Context
Existing conditions, orientation, climate, and daily movement define the first limits of the project.

Structure
Proportion, circulation, and order turn early observations into a spatial framework.

Material
Surfaces are chosen for touch, ageing, restraint, and their relationship to light.

Light
Light is composed as part of the architecture, shaping rhythm, depth, and stillness.

Atmosphere
The final space is measured by how calmly it supports life, movement, and memory.
```

---

## Transition Language

All transitions should feel like one continuous process.

The section should not cut between six unrelated slides. Each state should inherit something from the previous state.

Transition order:

```txt
Research -> Context
Loose traces become place.

Context -> Structure
Place becomes order.

Structure -> Material
Order becomes touch.

Material -> Light
Touch becomes depth.

Light -> Atmosphere
Depth becomes feeling.
```

---

## Final Story Principle

The experience should communicate one idea:

```txt
Architecture is not produced by form first.
It emerges through attention.
```

If the visitor remembers only the animation, the section has failed.

If the visitor understands that the studio designs through observation, restraint, and atmosphere, the section has succeeded.
