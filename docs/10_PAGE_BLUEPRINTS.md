# 10 — Page Blueprints

## Purpose

This document defines the structural blueprint for every page of the website.

It focuses on:

* storytelling
* composition
* visual rhythm
* content hierarchy
* user experience
* motion opportunities

This document does not define UI.

It defines the experience.

---

# HOME

## Goal

Create an emotional first impression.

Introduce the studio through atmosphere rather than explanation.

The homepage should encourage visitors to explore projects.

---

## Section 01

### Hero

Purpose

Create emotion immediately.

Content

* Fullscreen photography or cinematic video
* Editorial headline
* Minimal navigation
* Scroll indicator

Motion

* Smooth fade-in
* Text reveal
* Slow image scale
* Lenis smooth scrolling

---

## Section 02

### Selected Projects

Purpose

Immediately demonstrate quality.

Content

4–6 featured projects.

Large editorial cards.

No long descriptions.

Motion

Image reveal.

Hover preview.

Subtle parallax.

---

## Section 03

### Studio Introduction

Purpose

Introduce the philosophy.

Content

* Short editorial statement
* One supporting paragraph
* Link to Studio page

Motion

Fade.

Reveal.

No dramatic animation.

---

## Section 04

### Featured Project

Purpose

Slow the pace.

Immerse the visitor.

Content

One project.

Large photography.

Minimal text.

Motion

Pinned section (optional).

Slow image transition.

---

## Section 05

### Contact

Purpose

Finish calmly.

Email

Location

Social links

# PAGE RHYTHM

Every page should alternate between:

Large image

↓

Whitespace

↓

Typography

↓

Whitespace

↓

Large image

↓

Editorial statement

↓

Whitespace

Never stack too many text sections.

---

# MOTION RHYTHM

Animation should follow the user's attention.

Reveal.

Pause.

Reveal.

Pause.

Never animate everything simultaneously.

---

# CONTENT RHYTHM

Photography first.

Typography second.

Metadata third.

Interface last.

---

# PAGE TRANSITIONS

Transitions should feel like walking through the same building.

Moving from one page to another should feel natural.

No flashy transitions.

Only calm cinematic movement.

---

# Final Principle

Every page should tell a story.

Not simply display information.

The visitor should remember the sequence of spaces rather than the sequence of sections.
